What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body processes blood sugar (glucose). There are two main types: Type 1, where the body produces little or no insulin, and Type 2, where the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn’t produce enough.

Why Track Your Data?

Tracking your glucose levels, meals, medications, and exercise helps identify trends, improve treatment decisions, and avoid complications. Studies show better tracking leads to better blood sugar control.

Blood Glucose Target Ranges

Normal blood glucose targets (may vary by person):

  • Before meals: 80–130 mg/dL
  • 1–2 hours after meals: less than 180 mg/dL
Always follow your healthcare provider’s specific recommendations.

Healthy Lifestyle Tips

- Choose fiber-rich foods and avoid processed sugars
- Move regularly (even walking helps!)
- Stay hydrated and get enough sleep
- Keep stress in check — it affects blood sugar, too!

Signs of High or Low Blood Sugar

Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia): shakiness, sweating, dizziness, confusion
High Blood Sugar (Hyperglycemia): thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurry vision
